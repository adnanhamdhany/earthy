Indonesia Is the Second Largest Plastic Waste Contributor to the Ocean

Indonesia produces more than 3 million tons of plastic waste every year that ends up in the ocean. Rivers such as the Citarum, Brantas, and Bengawan Solo play a major role in transporting plastic waste from land to sea.

Deforestation in Indonesia Is Decreasing — But Not Over

Indonesia has successfully reduced deforestation rates in recent years through forest moratorium policies and conservation programs. However, illegal logging and land conversion still threaten biodiversity, especially in Kalimantan and Papua. Protecting forests means protecting wildlife, indigenous communities, and our climate.

The Citarum River: From Life Source to One of the Most Polluted Rivers

The Citarum River in West Java supplies water for agriculture, industry, and daily life for millions of people. However, industrial waste, household trash, and poor waste management have made it one of the most polluted rivers in the world. Government programs and community clean-up movements have started to improve the situation, showing that collective action truly matters.

Eco-Friendly Living Hack You Can Start Today

  • Bring your own reusable shopping bag to reduce plastic waste.
  • Use refill stations for drinking water instead of buying bottled water.
  • Separate organic and non-organic waste at home.
  • Support local products to reduce carbon emissions from transportation.
